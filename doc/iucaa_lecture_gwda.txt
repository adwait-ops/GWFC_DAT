Lecture 1: Statistical Foundations + Detector Response
--------------------------------------------------------

Core Topics:

Data model: x(t) = n(t) + h(t)
Random variables: multivariate Gaussian distributions
Noise characterization: stationarity (WSS), PSD
Autocorrelation and power spectral density
Noise-weighted inner product and Hilbert space view

Advanced Topics:

Covariance matrix as metric, inverse covariance
Whitening operator and geometric interpretation
PSD estimation (Welch, median, windowing effects)
Non-Gaussian noise and glitches
Breakdown of stationarity

Detector Response:

h(t) = Fp hp + Fc hc
Antenna pattern functions Fp, Fc
Sky dependence (RA/Dec), polarization angle
Detector sensitivity and blind spots
Parameter dependence h(t; theta, \hat n, \psi)

Tutorial:

Generate Gaussian noise (white vs colored)
Estimate PSD from data
Compute covariance matrix
Plot antenna patterns over the sky


Lecture 2: Matched Filtering and Optimal Detection
--------------------------------------------------------

Core Topics:

Detection problem: H0 (noise) vs H1 (signal + noise)
Matched filter derivation
Signal-to-noise ratio (SNR)
Frequency-domain implementation (FFT)
Optimality of matched filtering

Advanced Topics:

Variational derivation of matched filter
Likelihood interpretation: log \Lambda = (x,h) - (1/2)(h,h)
Maximization over phase and time
Template normalization
Effect of waveform mismatch
Robustness issues in non-Gaussian noise

Detector/Network Aspect:

Detector-dependent signal: h_d(t) = Fp^d hp + Fc^d hc
Same source, different projections across detectors

Tutorial:

Implement matched filtering using FFT
Inject CBC waveform and recover SNR
Study mismatch effects
Compare whitening vs non-whitening
Two-detector signal comparison
